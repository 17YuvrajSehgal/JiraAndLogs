\subsection{Where the lift came from}
\label{sec:results:progression}

Three refinements were integrated into the cascade after the initial specification was locked. Table~\ref{tab:cumulative} reports the cumulative effect of each, integrated in sequence. The pattern is the most informative finding of the project.

\begin{table*}[t]
  \centering
  \caption{Cumulative effect of the three integrated refinements. Each row adds one refinement to the row above. Retrieval and triage metrics are essentially fixed from row two onward; the project's full empirical lift is in the novelty-recall column.}
  \label{tab:cumulative}
  \small
  \setlength{\tabcolsep}{8pt}
  \begin{tabular}{@{}lrrrrr@{}}
    \toprule
    Stage & Hit@1 & Hit@5 & MRR & PR-AUC inclusive & Novel recall \\
    \midrule
    Initial cascade                       & 0.707 & 0.912 & 0.788 & 0.853 & 0.163 \\
    \;\;$+$ bi-encoder mixed negatives    & 0.722 & 0.912 & 0.794 & 0.856 & 0.161 \\
    \;\;$+$ complete agent coverage       & 0.722 & 0.912 & 0.794 & 0.856 & 0.356 \\
    \;\;$+$ learned novelty classifier    & 0.722 & 0.912 & 0.794 & 0.856 & \textbf{0.793} \\
    \bottomrule
  \end{tabular}
\end{table*}

The first refinement adds one truly random negative per anchor to the bi-encoder's fine-tuning curriculum, alongside the original hard negatives mined by sparse retrieval. This forces semantic discrimination beyond lexical overlap and produces a modest lift on the top-one position; the other channels are unchanged. The second refinement completes the language-model agent's coverage of the test set: where the agent had previously run only on a strategic subset of windows, extending it to the full set roughly doubles the novelty channel's recall. The third refinement, contributing the bulk of the headline win, replaces the fixed retrieval-confidence threshold inside the novelty layer with a learned per-window classifier conditioned on coarse window metadata; this lifts novel recall by a further factor of 2.2 at unchanged precision.

The structural observation that emerges from Table~\ref{tab:cumulative} is that the retrieval channel (Hit@1, Hit@5, MRR) is essentially fixed across every refinement: every metric in the retrieval columns is identical from row 2 onward. The project's full empirical lift comes from the novelty channel. We had originally framed the work as a retrieval-improvement project; the data shows it is more accurately a novelty-detection project. The retrieval channel was already at a local optimum --- the union of the four chosen retrievers' top-five lists contains the gold ticket on 97.6\% of windows, and the cascade's 0.912 sits at 93.5\% of that union ceiling --- whereas the novelty channel had been resting on a fixed-threshold heuristic that was leaving most of its addressable signal on the table.

\subsection{Three rerankers, three negative results}
\label{sec:results:rerankers}

Three independent attempts were made to improve the top-one retrieval slot beyond the cascade's overlap-rerank, each with a different mechanism, each integrated into the cascade in the natural place a reranker would go. All three underperformed the cascade. The pattern is consistent enough to be reported as a finding in its own right.

The first attempt fine-tuned a cross-encoder, which reads query and document jointly so that token-level interactions a bi-encoder cannot see become available. Integrated as a fifth retriever in the fusion, it lowered Hit@1 by 5.4 percentage points; integrated as a reranker over the cascade's top-five, it lowered Hit@1 by 4.5 percentage points. The second attempt allowed the language-model agent to override the cascade's top-one pick when its self-rated confidence exceeded a threshold. Audited on a 200-window sample, the agent was right when the cascade was wrong on three windows, and wrong when the cascade was right on eight --- a net loss of five top-one wins. The third attempt invoked a separate large-language-model judge over the cascade's top-five output with a strict JSON output schema and confidence-gated override of the top-one. The judge's confidence distribution turned out to be uninformative (more than 80\% of calls reported a confidence near 0.85, regardless of whether the candidate was correct), and every override threshold tested cost between 2.7 and 13 points of Hit@1.

The three designs are mechanistically distinct---a re-encoded joint score, an LLM verification stage, an LLM scoring stage---but the negative result is identical. We discuss its interpretation in \S\ref{sec:discussion}; here we simply report that on this dataset, multi-retriever consensus on the top-one slot resists every reranker we tried.

\subsection{Robustness}
\label{sec:results:robustness}

Robustness was measured against two forms of noise: distractor tickets injected into the retrieval corpus, and entire scenario families held out of training.

\paragraph{Memory noise.} We injected distractor tickets into the retrieval corpus at four ratios and measured the relative degradation. At a 10\% distractor ratio, Hit@5 drops by 0.3\% and Hit@1 by 0.4\% (both within bootstrap noise). At 25\%, Hit@5 drops 1.3\% and Hit@1 drops 8.8\%. At 50\%, Hit@5 drops only 2.0\% relative to the clean baseline; Hit@1 is more sensitive at 14.2\% relative. The relative sensitivity ranking is consistent across ratios: Hit@5 $\gg$ MRR $>$ Hit@1, reflecting that distractors can displace the top pick without disturbing the top-five coverage that the cascade's RRF rule defends.

\paragraph{Family shift.} Under leave-one-family-out cross-validation (twenty-seven folds, each holding out a complete scenario family from training and evaluating only on that family), the novelty F1 at the best operating point comes in at 0.788, which is 11.4\% relative below the in-distribution figure of 0.890. The headline novel-recall win survives the shift: even under family hold-out, the cascade's novel recall (0.783) exceeds the initial cascade's in-distribution recall (0.163) by 381\% relative. Precision is robust across most families --- nineteen of twenty-seven families exceed 0.90 precision at the headline threshold --- while recall splits sharply by family structure: families dominated by pre-fault or recovery windows generalize perfectly, while families dominated by active-fault windows lose 30 to 50 points of recall when held out, consistent with the learned classifier exploiting active-fault metadata that does not transfer to families it has not seen.

\subsection{Engineer utility}
\label{sec:results:utility}

Under the serial-candidate-review simulation defined in \S\ref{sec:evaluation:metrics}, the cascade reduces the expected time-to-diagnose per resolvable incident by approximately six to seven minutes relative to the detection-only baseline that produces no retrieval output. Across the scale of incidents a typical on-call queue handles per week, this is the difference between an engineer who spends most of their time investigating familiar incidents and one who has the budget to investigate the genuinely novel ones. The cascade itself runs at roughly sixteen microseconds per window after the per-pipeline predictions are cached, so the engineering-minutes saved are net of the cascade's own runtime cost.
